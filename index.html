THE WHAT

In the online world, algorithms are used by companies to process data, make decisions, and tailor experiences for users. They power everything from search engine results and social media feeds to product recommendations and targeted advertising.

You might be wondering... When do I even give them the information to know what I might like?

Signing up for an account can tell them your age and gender. Collaborative filtering filters out items that a user might like on the basis of reactions by similar users. They take account of your search history, likes, shares, comments, clicks. Even the time of day you use the app website tells them something about you.

THE WHY

Seeing the same content over and over again puts you in a bubble that can allow for social media addiction and misinformation to spread. Companies care about one thing: Revenue. They are always trying to sell you something even when you donâ€™t realize it.

THE SOLUTION?

After learning about how algorithms can affect your online experience in a negative way, you might be thinking about how to fix it. Although it is difficult to completely escape them, there are ways to mitigate the negative affects.

Actively seeking out other perspectives, reducing your intake of social media, and keeping yourself educated and informed about the impact of algorithms and the companies that take advantage of it.
